                          AN OVERVIEW OF STATE CODES

Logic states:

There are three "basic" states that any node may contain:

   "1", "high", or "on"

   "0", "low", or "off"

   "X", "indeterminate", or "undefined"

The first two are well understood, but the indeterminate state is less so.
It indicates that either a high, low, or even invalid state exists.
Suprisingly, a great deal of processing can occur based on this state, and
the undefined state plays a very important role in evaluation of the reset
condition, since undefines must be handled in the real world, too.

Transistor states:

Even represented as ideal switches, transistors have their own unique state
properties. These are determined by the state, as above, that appears on
the gate of the transistor:

   "on" or "driven"

   "off" or "high impedance"

   "partial on" or "indeterminate drive"

To find the actual states that may appear in a circuit, the following
example applies:


                          G
                          |
                       -------
                       |     |
                       S     D

Givien the three logical states, high, low, and indeterminate, appearing
on the gate and source, what will the state be on the output, the drain, if
source and drain were the same before the current gate state ?
The answer is the product of all logical states appearing at the source,
with the states appearing at the gate, or 9 different types. It is important,
however, to realize that since a gate does not care what type of drive
is present, all these states still resolve into the original logical states:

                                      gate
   Source         on            off                  indeterminate
   ----------------------------------------------------------------
   High           high          stored high          indeterminate high
   low            low           stored low           indeterminate low
   indeterminate  indeterminate stored indeterminate indeterminate

Or, grouped by logical states:

                               high
                                |
      -------------------------------------------------------
      |                         |                           |
      high             indeterminate high              stored high

                               low
                                |
      -------------------------------------------------------
      |                         |                           |
      low              indeterminate low               stored low

                           indeterminate
                                |
      -------------------------------------------------------
      |                         |                           |
      indeterminate        indeterminate               stored indeterminate

How can an indeterminate or unknown be high or low ? Actually, an
indeterminate high is still not a valid high, an could even be a low.
It is important to separate it from a "normal" indeterminate because
it has very special properties in an actual circuit. The reason is that
the "low" component of an indeterminate high can only be stored. This means
that it can be made into a valid high by another driven node.
Note also that there are only two types of indeterminates, stored and
driven. Although they do have different properties, we treat them the same.
In fact, to understand what a driven indeterminate means, we must examine
how one comes about. Since a stored state can never be used to produce drive,
and we assume only valid rails are used, a driven indeterminate can only
come about one way. This is by mixing driven high and low levels.
Our name for a pure driven indeterminate, is, therefore, contention.
We also recognize a special version of a stored indeterminate, known as
undefined, which represents the value of all nodes on reset. This allows
tracking of node sets, and is referred to as a "diagnostic" state.

Weak states:

To enable representation of all actual circuit states, we also include a
set of "alternate" driven states known as weak states. The rules for the
weak states are just as the strong states:

                                      gate
   Source         on            off                  indeterminate
   ----------------------------------------------------------------
   weak high      weak high     stored high          weak ind high
   Weak low       weak low      stored low           weak ind low
   weak ind       weak ind      stored indeterminate weak ind

Or, grouped by logical states:

                            weak high
                                |
      -------------------------------------------------------
      |                         |                           |
      weak high             weak ind high              stored high

                               low
                                |
      -------------------------------------------------------
      |                         |                           |
      weak low              weak ind low               stored low

                         weak indeterminate
                                |
      -------------------------------------------------------
      |                         |                           |
      weak ind           weak indeterminate            stored indeterminate

Note that only the driven states need be represented specially for weak.
A stored charge does not care how it came to be placed.

The states:

The resulting states as used in the simulator are:

   1. high.
   2. low.
   3. Indeterminate (contention).
   4. stored high.
   5. stored low.
   6. stored indeterminate.
   7. indeterminate high.
   8. indeterminate low.
   9. weak high.
   10. weak low.
   11. weak indeterminate (weak contention).
   12. weak indeterminate high.
   13. weak indeterminate low.
   14. Undefined.
   15. power.
   16. ground.

These last states exist for control purposes. Sixteen states is an opportune
number for processing. A transistor can be "evaluated" by the simulator
by simply performing a 256 address lookup by the 8 bit byte formed by the
conbination of the source and drain states.
