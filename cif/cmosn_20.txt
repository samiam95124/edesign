			CMOSN_20A LIBRARY				
		 Scalable CMOS Standard Cell Library

Reference: IEEE 1986 Custom Integrated Circuits Conference
Proceedings, 'A High Performance, Multisourceable, Scalable CMOS Cell
Family' by Robert C.  Newton and Mark D. Lantz

	This is a scalable, double metal CMOS standard cell library
featuring 50 SSI and 28 MSI-level macrocells with typical gate delays
of less than 1 ns, and up to 32K bits of RAM/ROM.  Characterization
over the entire military range indicates that the library can realize
75 MHz designs at 1.2 microns with three levels of logic between
clocked elements and three fanouts per cell output.  A double-height
cell structure was chosen to optimize the layout for the MSI-level
macrocells, which are available in bit increments of 4, 8, and 16 with
shift registers in sizes up to 32 bits.  All I/O terminals are 4
lambda wide on a 10 lambda grid in second-layer metal.

	Chip designs generated using this library at 2.0 micron
scaling can be fabricated with the MOSIS NSCE, NSCN and NSCP 0.8 lambda
wafer runs, and designs at 1.2 micron scaling can be fabricated with
MOSIS NSCE and NSCN 0.6 lambda wafer runs.

	NOTE: These technologies (NSC*) are not compatible with normal
MOSIS scalable technologies (SC*).  You cannot mix layout made with
incompatible design rules on the same chip!!!

This library is available only on tape with a signed license agreement
and a tape processing fee.  Included are programs for VAX/VMS and
Berkeley Unix compatible systems to generate the RAM and ROM blocks.

Cell descriptions
-----------------

The cells can be divided into six groups: COMBINATORIAL, STORAGE, MSI,
RAM/ROM, PAD, and FIDUCIARY/TEST.  Cell heights of the COMBINATORIAL,
STORAGE, and MSI groups are a constant 250 lambda.  I/O terminals are
in METAL2 on both the top and bottom edges of these cells; power
terminals are in METAL1 on the left and right edges.  Only one side of
each I/O and power terminal needs to be connected.

The MSI cells have subcells that you can combine or iterate for
different bit sizes, such as to make a 12-bit shift-register.  The
final block will obey the I/O conventions of the combinatorial cells.

The RAM/ROM subcells have to be carefully chosen and positioned, for
which a program is supplied to both build the RAM/ ROM blocks and
generate their timing characteristics and footprint (boundary
description).  I/O terminals for these blocks are placed on the bottom
edge, while power terminals are on the left and right edges.  (Again,
only one side of each connector need be connected to a power source.)
For the ROM block, however, there are additional input connectors on
the left and right edges of the block, BOTH of which need to be
connected.  The program allows some user control of aspect ratio.

The PAD cells are either 413 or 550 lambdas tall, respectively, for 2
micron and 1.2 micron designs.  (Pads are not generally scalable
because of the design rule requirement of a minimum metal bonding pad
size in microns).  Their terminals are in METAL2 on the bottom edge.
To fit pads into a frame small enough for the 2um Tinychip size (see
the appendix on standard frames and Tinychips), there must be fewer
than the maximum of 40 pads, but as long as they are spaced evenly
around the periphery of the chip (to insure bondability) and the chip
size is correct, the chip will qualify as a Tinychip.

The FIDUCIARY/TEST cells include level identifiers, via cells, and
test structures which are not required for MOSIS fabrication.  MOSIS
maintains its own parametric test structures for purposes of wafer
acceptance and tracking, and publishes typical SPICE level 2 and/or
BSIM transistor models for each run.
